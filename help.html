Help & FAQ

How do I use the Live Tracker? +
Go to Live Tracker. Step 1: Search and click your boarding bus stop. Step 2: Search and click your destination stop. Step 3: The system shows buses connecting those two stops with predicted ETAs. Click "View on map" to see the route highlighted.
How does GPS work? +
Click the "📍 Use GPS" button. Your browser will ask for location permission — click Allow. The system will find the nearest bus stop to your current location and select it automatically as your boarding stop.
Why does it say "No direct bus found"? +
This means no single bus runs directly between the two stops you selected. In the demo, only stops on the same route are connected. Try choosing stops from the same route: Shivajinagar↔Swargate (Bus 101), Katraj↔Hadapsar (Bus 155), or Wakad↔Kothrud (Bus 50).
Which stops are on which route? +
Bus 101: Shivajinagar → FC Road → Deccan → Nal Stop → Dandekar Bridge → Swargate

Bus 155: Katraj → Bibwewadi → Swargate → Market Yard → Pune Railway Station → Ghorpadi → Hadapsar

Bus 50: Wakad → Baner Road → Aundh IT Park → Parihar Chowk → Karve Road → Kothrud Depot
How is the ETA calculated? +
The ETA has two parts: (1) Wait time — how long until the next bus arrives at your boarding stop, calculated from the bus schedule. (2) Ride time — distance between your stops divided by average bus speed, plus 1.5 min dwell time per stop, multiplied by a traffic factor based on the current hour.
Why does traffic affect ETA? +
Pune has predictable peak-hour traffic. The system detects the current time and applies a delay multiplier: peak hours (8–10am, 5–8pm) add 40% more time, off-peak is normal, and night hours are 15% faster. This makes ETAs much more realistic than a simple distance/speed calculation.
How do I run this locally? +
The frontend works without any server — just open index.html in your browser.

For the Flask backend:
1. pip install flask flask-cors scikit-learn numpy pandas
2. python train_model.py (run once)
3. python app.py
4. Open index.html in browser.
Can I reset and search again? +
Yes. After viewing your journey, scroll down in the left panel and click "← New Journey". Or click "Change" next to your stop selections to go back a step.
